About Me

I am originally from Maharashtra, India and am currently pursuing my M.S./Ph.D. at University of Massachusetts, Amherst in the field of human-aligned robotics. I hold a B.Tech. in Electrical Engineering from Indian Institue of Technology, Madras.

Before joining UMass, I was working in Adobe as an ML research associate where I primarily focused on reinforcement learning (RL) systems for automated decision making. Prior to that, I have also worked as an ML engineer where I deployed and maintained models for Adobe's form conversion service.

My current research interest lies in deploying RL-based systems, such as robots, in a safe and human aligned manner. At UMass, I have the fortune to work under Prof. Scott Niekum, and to closely work with Prof. Bruno Castro's and Prof. Phil Thomas' group, too.

In my pastime, I read books, paint, and explore places nearby. You can find my reading list here. A few of my paintings can be found here. And as for my adventurous explorations, Amherst being one of the most beautiful places not just in US, but in the world, I hop on a public bus and visit scenic places in the neighbourhood. Very recently, I went on a hike on a cliff-side near Clark Mountain. I also visited the pristine Connecticut River. In future, I am looking forward to trek on Mt. Sugarloaf which is very close to my residence. Eventually, I want to visit world famous all-American places such as Niagara Falls, NYC and LA.
