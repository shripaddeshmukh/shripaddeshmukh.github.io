About Me

I am a first-year MS/PhD student at University of Massachusetts, Amherst. I am originally from Maharashtra, India. My broad research interests lie in developing deployable sequential decision-making systems. Here at UMass, I have privelege to work under Prof. Scott Niekum. I also enjoy working closely with Prof. Bruno Castro's and Prof. Phil Thomas' group.

Prior to joining UMass, I used to work at Adobe (2020-2023) where I primarily focused on reinforcement learning (RL) systems for automated decision making. I have had the fortune to work with Late Georgios Theocharous and Prof. Nan Jiang on a problem in RL deployability. I have dabbled in computer vision and a bit of natural language processing as well. I worked on problems in document analysis and automated form conversion, where I gained hands-on experience with training and maintaining large neural networks. Prior to that, I completed my Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology Madras in 2020.

In my leisure time, I like to read books, paint, write, and explore nearby places. You can find my reading list here. A few of my paintings can be found here. My blogs can be found here. As for my adventurous explorations, just before arriving in US I went bike-riding in the lower Himalayas of northern India, and I am looking forward to exploring the US. I have been on a few hikes at Amherst on nearby mountains on the banks of pristine Connecticut river.
