Thoughts on circles one hangs out with

Uploaded on October 7th, 2023.

A brief context

I have moved from places to places in my life. From my village to Solapur, then Solapur to Chennai and later to Delhi for work. Recently, I moved to UMass for my Ph.D. I have met many people, lived with different set of folks, have had both good and not so good interactions. In this blog, I just wanted to jot down few thoughts about how such change of groups affects oneself individually, and few random ideas about how that would be working, and if there is any golden way to get along with folks. (This is not a scientific write-up, rather, a personal story telling.)

The story begins...

For those of you who don't know what an equilibrium means, when multiple people interact and work together, there comes a state where everyone defaults to a certain behaviour. For instance, if you are with family members, you are a totally different person, and when you are with your work place colleagues, you act in a way that is 'professional'. If you have multiple friend circles, particularly, if you are like me, who has to move places to places, with a switch of context, your behaviour defaults to a way that is special for that particular circle with which you are hanging out. Many a times, you are held in very high regard in one of these circles and you would want to be equally regarded in the ones you meet in the future, or you do not want to be associated with a particular ideology of circle you have been part of (in)voluntarily. So, how does it work? How does one carry the image you want to present consistently across multiple groups without offending anyone or being seen as rude?

From my personal experience, it is very dependent on the time you are going to spend in a new circle and how much idea they have about you, your work, your accomplishments, etc., already. In an inter-team collaboration, you hardly get time to form good rapport with those you are working with. I have seen research collaboration, where you drop by every other week to ideate and take/give updates. It is impossible to make any impression on the folks involved. But, for this blog, we will not talk about these shorter interactions. I am more interested in understanding how a person's moves to-and-from different circles affects him/her individually and how his/her self-image changes and how default behaviours form.

Let's talk about the importance of group you have to hang out with. The question is how does quality of people around you affect your personal behaviour? To begin with, I will give an example of my class in Solapur. The batch I was part of, which completed high school education in 2014, was one of the exceptional batches. The reasons being -- everyone was very hard working, very sincere, smart, and obidient to teachers. When I moved to this school from my village, I was amazed by how good these folks were and always thought of myself as a fortunate guy to get to interact with these folks and learn with them. My own behaviour became reciprocative to these high-performing individuals and overall, I feel, the whole foundation of my future achievements was laid here. Not to mention, all these awesome folks are doing great in their lives today. So, let's recap. A good student moves to a circle that values higher qualities in a student, he improves himself and changes totally.

Now comes the reason why I started writing this blog. Not always I have associated with better people. Sometimes in ignorance and sometimes due to the work and education constraints, I had to mix with folks with whom I really shouldn't have mixed, at least in the retrospect. Although you want to act in a very amenable, healthy kind of way with those around you, if the other person's behaviour always hurts your own self-image and distorts your ideal scenario, you are struggling with circle transition. Some folks just do not want to see you in the way you see yourself although you deserve to be seen that way. Yes, there are caveats. If somebody wants me to see him/her as the most funny person and I find it difficult to rank them as the funniest, I should not simply assume him/her to funny unrealistically. However, if that person is really good at humour and you can see him/her as next great comedian, you should at least appreciate it. The inspiration from your group really helps the individuals grow their self-images, do great things and contribute overall.

I am not perfect either. I know my past mistakes where I have not acted in a way where I have encouraged good in others. To mend things, I have started noting down places where I could have, as a part of circle, done much better. But, at the same time, I also take notes of how the circle around me is changing my behaviour and if that's in a good way. The later part is important -- How does one's behaviour change for good? Who defines what is good? Are there any guidelines on what to expect from a group? The answers to these questions are as diverse as it gets. My thumb rule is to see whether I am happy hanging out with the folks and whether I am able to pursue higher things which I want to pursue. If not, then something needs to change for sure. Seeking better circle is always advantageous. Again, better for me is where I am happy and where the people in the circle really care about what I want to do with my life.

The conclusion is...

The circle you are currently in, changes your default behaviour for both good and bad. The equilibrium reached has to be beneficial to everyone. Otherwise, we need to change something.
